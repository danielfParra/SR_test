Instructions

Today, you will be randomly paired with another participant to play a game. The computer has randomly assigned you the role of Player A or Player B, which you will keep for the {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds of the game. We will tell you your role after we have explained the game. In each round, if you are a Player A, you will be randomly matched with a Player B, and vice versa. Your partner will change randomly from one round to the next. Neither of you will know the identity of the other, and all interactions will be anonymized by the computers.

This is a game where Player A sends a message to Player B, who then guesses a secret number. The payoffs for both players depend on Player B's guess and the secret number.

How the Game Works

Each round of the game has four steps.

  1. The computer generates a secret number, which is 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7.

  2. Player A sends a message to Player B saying "To receive the highest payoff, you should guess X." Player A can choose any number 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, or 7 for X. You will learn more about Player A's exact choice options below.

  3. Player B observes the message and guesses the secret number. Player B can guess any number between 1 and 7, including two-digit numbers. For example, a Player B who thinks that 1 and 2 are equally likely can guess 1.50, and a Player B who thinks that the secret number is either 4 or 5 but most likely 5 can make a guess between 4 and 5 but closer to 5, such as 4.8. {{ if player.participant.treatment == "Decode" }}Sometimes, Player B will have to decode the message before making a guess. You will learn more about this later.{{ endif }}

  4. Both Player A and Player B will receive feedback. This feedback will include the secret number generated by the computer, Player A's message, and Player B's guess.

How does the computer generate the secret number?
You can think of the computer as a device that randomly selects one of seven balls from an urn that are numbered 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.


As the urn contains one ball of each number, the probability of generating any given number between 1 and 7 is equal. Consequently, you can reasonably assume that the secret number will, on average, be equal to 4.

These draws are completely independent across rounds. This means that you should not strategize about the value of the secret number based on the value of secret number in a previous round.

Does Player A know the secret number when sending the message?
No, Player A does not know the secret number when sending the message.

What can Player A choose to send?
Player A can choose any number between 1 and 7 to send as a message to Player B. For example, if Player A chooses “2”, Player B will receive the message "To receive the highest payoff, you should guess 2." {{ if player.participant.treatment != "Babbling" }}

Instead of sending a specific number, Player A can also delegate the decision to the computer who transmits the true secret number to Player B. If Player A chooses this option, Player B will receive the message "To receive the highest payoff, you should guess X", where the computer will replace X with the true secret number it generated at the start of the round. Since the message format stays the same, Player B will never know whether Player A chose to delegate the decision to the computer or not. {{ endif }}
